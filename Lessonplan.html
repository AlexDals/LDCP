Learning Dals Connect PRO

OVERVIEW & PURPOSE

This course will go over all the necessary steps to become a pro at connecting and configuring Dals Connect PRO products. This course will be split into 3 main sections, connection, configuration and a specific section dedicated to the PRO Hub.

OBJECTIVES

  1. Being able to connect every Dals Connect PRO products
  2. Become acquainted with Dals Connect PRO configurations
  3. Learn everything about the PRO Hub

SYNOPSIS

Dals Connect PRO was launched in 2022 and is using a new type of communication called bluetooth low energy mesh. This move was made in response to larger scale projects wanting to use Dals Connect products which had a limited range and number of active devices on most networks. Here are the main reasons to choose Dals Connect PRO:

  1. Unlimited number of devices
    There are almost no physical limits to how many bluetooth mesh connected devices you can have on a BLE mesh network. The number is so high that you could light a small village on one single bluetooth mesh network.
  2. Unlimited range
    Contrary to DALS Connect products, which uses Wi-Fi technology, the range on BLE mesh is practically unlimited. As long as another BLE enabled device is in range, that device can relay the message to another device and so forth, until it reaches the desired destination.
  3. Control your devices locally, or through the cloud
    Bluetooth Low Energy mesh networking works on the basics of flooding. This means that on their first use, a BLE mesh device will send the instructions to all other BLE mesh devices nearby for them to either accept the instructions if it is meant for them, or relay the instructions to other nearby devices and so forth. This means that no internet connection is required in your own home to control your Dals Connect PRO products.  If you wish to use voice assistants and remote control, the PRO hub is there to help you.

1. Connection

In this activity you will learn how to connect your Dals Connect PRO devices to your Dals Connect application.

Resetting

All Dals Connect PRO products are based on the BLE mesh protocol. This means that the number of ON/OFF required to reset a device is different from Dals Connect products.

First connection

When you first connect a Dals Connect PRO product, the product will be set to Warm White (around 3000K temperature) and 100% brightness. They will not be pairable in this state.  To initiate the first connection, we have to turn the light ON/OFF 3 times.

Once the light breathes, this means that the device is ready for connection. Make sure to have your Dals Connect app ready. If you have a newer phone, the product should pop right away at the bottom.

If it doesn’t, just go to auto-scan and the product should be detected within a mere seconds.

Once the steps are complete, your Dals Connect PRO product is ready to be controlled from your phone*.

*Be aware that since this is a bluetooth connection, only 1 phone at a time can control your smart device. 

Network Reset

Once your device has been connected to your Dals Connect app, it is now considered configured and will no longer reset with only 3 ON/OFF. You will need to follow the steps below to get your device reset.

  1. Turn the light on
  2. Repeat the following sequence : OFF (count 1-2) - ON - OFF (count 1-2) - ON - OFF (count 1-2)- ON - OFF (count 1-2)- ON
  3. The device should start breathing in a warm white color, indicating that it has been reset.
  4. Repeat the same steps as during your first connection in the app to get the device paired. The device will have retained its name if you changed it initially.

2. Configuration

The Dals Connect app and the smart products themselves allow you to do quite a bit of automation even without an internet connection. Let’s go over the possibilities.

Local Control

You can use all of the RGB + CCT controls directly from your local connection with the smart product. As long as your bluetooth signal is within range of the smart device, it’ll accept commands. The optimal range for a bluetooth signal is within 10 meters, but some smartphones or tablets can communicate over a range of 20-30 meters without problem. You will have to test it yourself to define the optimal range.

Speaking of range, you can easily increase the range at which you control your Dals Connect PRO products by adding more products from the PRO family to your home. Remember as long as another BLE mesh device is in range, it’ll automatically relay the message to the next one if your phone signal doesn’t reach the device you want to control.

Sequences

You can control and program sequences as well as long as the device is within range. Once the sequence is set, the smart product will continue on the same sequence even after it has been turned off and back on.

Grouping

You will still be able to create groups even if your device is not connected to the cloud. You will however have to use the device information page to do so.

Once a group has been created, you can add another device to this group using the same group information page, or dismiss the group entirely.

3. Dals Connect PRO Hub

If you want to use voice assistants, timers and remote controls with your Dals Connect PRO product, you will need to acquire a Dals Connect PRO Hub. This hub acts as a gateway between your bluetooth mesh devices and the internet (the cloud). The hub itself also acts as a bluetooth mesh device, extending your signal range in your mesh network.

Connect

The Dals Connect PRO Hub is very easy to connect. Once the hub is plugged into its USB power supply, simply press the reset button on the back of the hub for 5 seconds, and let the button go. The hub network indicator should turn green and start flashing.

From this point, it should show up right away in your Dals Connect app.


If it doesn’t, it means that either your bluetooth is off on your phone, or you have an older smartphone and will need to use the “Auto-scan” feature like usual. From here, complete the setup like you normally do. Please note that even if the device is plugged in with an ethernet cable, the software will still ask for a Wi-Fi connection while being set up.

Configure

Once your hub is added to your Dals Connect app then you can start using it. On the main hub screen, we find a list of all available devices and 2 options at the bottom.

To add already paired Dals Connect PRO devices to your hub, you can use the “Add device by list” option. This option will list all PRO devices not currently paired to a hub and let you select them. Hit “Confirm” to add your products to the hub interface.

If you need to add a new device, you can do it one of two ways. The classic way is to use the “Search for new device” option at the bottom of the hub’s app panel. This will bring up the usual menu to add a new device.

The other method is to use the hub itself. On top of the hub, there is a “+” round button which you can press to pair any Dals Connect PRO devices. If the device has been reset and is in pairing mode, press the button on top of the hub to automatically pair them together.

Make sure the hub is placed close to your Wi-Fi router, or even better, hardwired to your router directly. A hardwire connection will guarantee that your hub is always connected even after a power failure.
